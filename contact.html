Stephen Foster

Director of Bands; Assistant Professor of Music
Stephen Foster joined the faculty of Faulkner University in the fall of 2014 as director of bands and assistant professor of music. He received his bachelor of music education degree from Middle Tennessee State University in both instrumental and school music. He also holds his master of music degree from Belmont University, along with an Ed. S. degree in Supervision and Instruction from Tennessee Technological University and an Ed. S. and Ed. D. from Union University. He has been teaching since 1984, with experience in public and private schools in Tennessee and Alabama. He has experience teaching kindergarten through college level students in general, vocal, and instrumental music education. Stephen is an active member of the National Association for Music Educators (NAfME), Alabama Bandmasters Association, and Alabama Music Educators Association. His major instrument is the trumpet.

Art Williams

Chair, Department of Fine Arts; Assistant Professor of Music
Art Williams joined the Faulkner University faculty as an assistant professor of music in the spring of 2014. He received his bachelor’s degree in music education and master’s degree in education from Troy University, and completed his Ph.D. degree in music education, with a minor field specialization in child development, from Indiana University in 2017. With experience teaching at the elementary, secondary and collegiate levels, Williams is a specialist in elementary general music education. He is also actively involved in the field of music education, where he appears as host of “Mr. Art’s Music Room,” the DVD component of Warner Brothers’ and Alfred Publications’ nationally released Expressions Music Curriculum.
An accomplished pianist, Williams’ research interests include the potential for music education through the media. Toward that end, he recently conducted a historical analysis of Fred Rogers’ methods of music education, as presented on the long-running public television program, Mister Rogers’ Neighborhood.
